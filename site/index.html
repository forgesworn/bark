Sign Nostr events with keys that never touch the browser

Bark bridges window.nostr to your remote NIP-46 signer: a Heartwood device, your own self-hosted heartwoodd bunker, or a hosted signer like nsec.app. Only signatures cross the wire.

Download View source

Free and open source. MIT licensed. No analytics, no accounts, no wallet.

Everything a signer extension should be

Bark does one job: let Nostr web apps request signatures without ever seeing your keys. Everything else exists to make that safe.

No keys in the browser

Bark never generates, stores, or touches private key material. Requests travel over encrypted NIP-46 to your signer, and only signatures come back.

Works with any NIP-46 bunker

nsecBunker, Amber, nsec.app, Heartwood, or your own signer. Pair by pasting a bunker URI, scanning a nostrconnect QR, or a local Heartwood address.

Policies down to method and kind

Per-site allow, ask, or deny rules with per-method and per-kind overrides. Profile, contacts, and relay-list events stay protected unless you say otherwise.

Privacy mode

Hide Bark from every site except the ones you whitelist. Unlisted pages cannot detect that a Nostr extension is installed at all.

Approvals that respect you

Nothing signs without your say. Allow once, trust a site for routine signing, or deny. Concurrent requests queue with a toolbar badge instead of failing.

Speaks your language

The interface ships in 53 languages, and the minimal permission footprint means the only Chrome permission Bark asks for is storage.

How it works

Three steps from install to your first signed event.

Install Bark

Add it to your browser from the store or load the release zip as an unpacked extension.

Pair your signer

Paste a bunker:// URI, scan a nostrconnect QR from the popup, or enter a local Heartwood address.

Approve and sign

Open any Nostr web app. Bark asks before a new site can use your identity; approve once or trust the site for routine signing.
