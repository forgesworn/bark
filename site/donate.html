Value for value

Bark is free. Not freemium, not free-until-we-raise, not free-in-exchange-for-your-data. Free, MIT licensed, and it stays that way.

Why we work this way

ForgeSworn is one developer, full time, building signing infrastructure for Nostr: this extension, the Heartwood hardware signer, the Android signer, the management tools, and a stack of open cryptography libraries underneath. No company, no token, no venture capital waiting for a return.

That model only works one way: if what we build is worth something to you, you send some of that value back. The price is whatever you think is honest, including nothing at all if times are tight. That's not a guilt trip; it's how value for value is supposed to work.

What your support actually funds

  • Store fees and accounts. Chrome, Firefox, and eventually Apple all charge for the privilege of shipping you free software.
  • Prototype hardware. Heartwood development means real ESP32 boards on a real bench, and the failed revisions you never see.
  • Test infrastructure. Relays, devices, and browsers for the e2e suites that keep a signing tool trustworthy.
  • Time. The honest one. Every hour funded is an hour on the next release instead of something else.
